Quick Start

Run Flow at a Glance

Use this section when you need the overall structure fast. Detailed rules below are authoritative for edge cases and exact limits.

01

Build the run

Choose a commander, draft 22 nonlands from your own 100-card pool, add lands, and apply any Shop or progression effects.

02

Fight encounters

Resolve Event, then Trinket before encounter 1. Fight encounters with shared party sequencing and d20 turn-order rolls.

03

Resolve rewards

On a win, resolve XP, Cash Out, and Loot Pool (all three must happen, no required order), then choose Town or Stay Out before Events.

04

Push to Crypt

Clear every encounter in the chosen mode, then beat the Crypt for Essence, Crypt Buffs, and long-term progression.

📜 Global Rules

  • Commander legality and color identity apply throughout the run.
  • The party plays cooperatively; the Host controls all encounters and opposing forces.
  • Persistent progression (Crypt Buffs, Tickets, Achievements, Brands, Captures) is applied before encounter 1.
  • Each player generates their own 100-card pool — only invalid or illegal individual cards are replaced.
  • Players fully reset to max HP before each new encounter branch.
  • Failed encounters skip XP, Cash Out, and Loot Pool only — the run still proceeds to Town or Stay Out.
  • Scryfall effects and pack generation follow normal legality and Host adjudication.

Host Reminders

  • Mode determines encounter count and whether Tier 4 affixes are active.
  • Host scaling uses player count, encounter stage, affix tier count, Doom, and Authority.
  • High roll on the pre-fight d20 chooses turn order.

Player Reminders

  • XP is run-only. Essence, Brands, Tickets, and other progression persist across runs.
  • No XP penalty when a player dies.

🚫 Banned and Restricted

These constraints apply across all MTGR runs unless the Host explicitly overrides them. Cards with Draft-only gameplay text (e.g. "Draft a card") are rerolled during deckbuilding — they are not legal in MTGR.

🚫 Full Ban

  • Show and Tell
  • Silence effects — any effect that prevents the Host from casting spells during their turn (exception: Mandate of Peace is legal)
  • Collective Voyage
  • Mana Abundance
  • Time Vault
  • Hermit Druid Avatar
  • Braids, Conjurer Adept Avatar
  • Staying Power
  • Rules Lawyer
  • Summoner's Bond
  • Richard Garfield, Ph.D.
  • Double Stroke
  • Sovereign's Realm

⚠️ Restricted

The following cards are legal but cannot be obtained via Scryfall search or any targeted card-search effect. They may still appear through random pack opens.

  • Join Forces (keyword) — cannot be fetched via Scryfall or targeted search
  • Oath of Lieges — cannot be fetched via Scryfall or targeted search
  • Scholarship Sponsor — cannot be fetched via Scryfall or targeted search
  • Zur's Weirding — cannot be fetched via Scryfall or targeted search
  • Tree of Perdition — cannot be used in combination with Balloon effects

📦 Resources

✨ Essence

Essence is the long-term resource. It persists between sessions and powers pre-game and post-game actions. Spend it outside the run to unlock options.

🔶 XP

XP is your in-run currency. Spend it on Town buildings and Wanderers. At run end — win or lose — unspent XP converts to Essence. Player death does not cause a base XP penalty.

🎒 Progression Inventory

Crypt Buffs, Tickets, Achievements, and Captures share 4 slots. Most items cost 1 slot — Vanguard Ticket costs 2, Emblem Ticket costs 3. Brands are slotless and uncapped.

🛒 Shop

Essence purchases available before Encounter 1 during deckbuilding. Brands may also be purchased here and carry forward across all runs.

Pregame Purchases (before Encounter 1)
  • 0 Essence — Commander Gamble: Mulligan your Commander for 1 new option. Your next Guild purchase this run is free.
  • 0 Essence — Partner / Background / etc.: Choose a Partner, Background, or similar pairing. If it says "Partner with X," you gain X instead.
  • 35 Essence — Land Replacement: Replace a basic land with a legal dual land or triome within your color identity. Repeatable.
  • 50 Essence — Commander Mulligan: Discard your current 5 Commander options and generate 5 new legal choices. Repeatable.
Brands (persistent — carry across all runs)

Brands are permanent upgrades. They do not occupy the 4-slot inventory. Each additional rank of the same Brand costs base price × current rank.

  • Brand of the Cartographer — 500 Essence base: When drafting, replace 2 basic lands with dual lands or triomes.
  • Brand of the Conclave — 1,000 Essence base: During deckbuilding and at The Guild, gain 1 additional Commander choice whenever Commanders are generated.
  • Brand of Recurrence — 1,250 Essence base: During deckbuilding and at The Guild, reroll Commanders 1 additional time for free.
  • Brand of the Open Hand — 1,750 Essence base: Whenever you open a pack, it contains 1 additional card to choose from.
  • Brand of the Blinded Eye — 2,000 Essence base: During deckbuilding, choose one color to exclude when rolling Commanders.
  • Brand of the Infinite Void — 500 Essence base: Your deck size minimum is reduced by 1 per Rank of this Brand.

🎴 Deckbuilding

Follow these steps in order at the start of each run. Initial deckbuilding is restricted to your Commander's color identity — cards outside it can only be gained during the run through cashouts, Mystery Packs, and other sources.

01

Choose Your Commander

Generate 5 random legal Commanders. You have up to 4 free mulligans to reroll the full set. Pick one.

02

Draft Your Cards

Generate your own 100-card pool within your color identity, then draft 22 nonlands from it. The pool cannot be rerolled as a whole — if an individual card is invalid, reroll and replace only that card.

03

Add Lands

Add 16 total lands. Duals by color count: 2 colors → 2 duals, 3 → 4, 4 → 6, 5 → 8. Fill the rest with basics.

04

Free Pick

Add 1 free-choice card that is Commander-legal, fits your color identity, and is not Sol Ring or a Gamechanger. Mark it with a Scryfall decal after selection.

05

Apply Progression

Finalize all persistent progression effects (Tickets, Brands, Shop purchases). Confirm the deck is Commander + 39 at minimum.

Deck Size — What Changes the Floor

  • Standard start: Commander + 39 cards minimum (40 total).
  • Can start larger: Ticket effects (Sol Ring Ticket, Arcane Signet Ticket, Trinket Ticket, etc.) add cards outside the 39 allocation — the deck may begin with more than 40 total.
  • Can drop below during the run: Brand of the Infinite Void reduces the minimum by 1 per Rank, so a deck can legally fall below its starting count mid-run.

Key Restrictions

  • Color identity applies at deckbuilding — no exceptions unless a progression effect explicitly grants off-color access.
  • The 100-card pool cannot be rerolled wholesale. Invalid individual cards are replaced one at a time.
  • Stickers, Attractions, and Contraptions cannot appear in the 100-card pool — reroll and replace any immediately.
  • The free pick cannot be Sol Ring, a Gamechanger, or a card banned in Commander.
  • No two-card combos are allowed in any run deck (players or Host).
  • A card marked with a Scryfall decal cannot be part of a three-card combo.

🎲 Gameplay Loop

Each run is a sequence of encounters separated by a Town or Stay Out choice, repeating until the Crypt is reached.

01

Before Encounter 1

Resolve Event first (opening Event cannot be Bad Stuff), then resolve Trinket using the Trinket System flow.

02

Encounter

Host draws a random encounter. Both sides roll a d20 — the higher roll chooses turn order.

03

Victory — Resolve Rewards

On a win, resolve all three in any order: XP (mode table + Affix bonuses), Cash Out, and Loot Pool.

03

Defeat — No Rewards

Failed encounters grant no XP, no Cash Out, and no Loot Pool. Skipping rewards is the only penalty — the run continues.

04

Between Encounters

After every encounter (win or loss), choose: 🏘️ Town or 🏕️ Stay Out. Resolve Events per that choice, then repeat from Step 02.

05

Crypt

Clear all encounters in the chosen run mode. The Crypt opens — Host always goes first inside.

🏘️ Town vs 🏕️ Stay Out

🏘️ Town

  • All players fully heal
  • Use Town buildings (limited uses per visit; all reset on entry)
  • Resolve 1 Event before the next encounter

Best for: controlled upgrades, building synergies, recovering safely.

🏕️ Stay Out

  • All players fully heal
  • Each player gains +10 XP (scales with consecutive Stay Outs)
  • Each player opens 2 Mystery Packs
  • Resolve a Supply Drop
  • Resolve a Wanderer
  • Resolve 2 Events (instead of 1; each additional consecutive Stay Out adds +1 more)

Best for: maximizing XP and cards, high-variance high-reward play.

Death & Scoop Rules
  • Death is not permanent — players return in future encounters.
  • No XP penalty when a player dies.
  • If a player leaves mid-encounter, the rest of the party may pilot their deck until that encounter ends.
  • Individual scoops — Sorcery speed only.
  • Full-party encounter concession — may be declared at Instant speed.

📘 Turn Structure (Party vs Host)

🛡️ Party Turn

All players act as one. One untap, upkeep, draw, main phase, combat, main phase 2, and end step. Coordinate sequencing and priority as a shared team.

👹 Host Turn

Host plays normally under the encounter's setup and scaling rules.

Extra turns belong only to the player who created them unless another rule explicitly overrides that.

🎲 Events

Events occur after post-encounter decisions and provide additional effects, challenges, or rewards before the next encounter begins. Events resolve one at a time in the order they are drawn. Each Event must fully complete before the next begins.

Event Timing

  1. Encounter is defeated.
  2. Rewards are resolved.
  3. Players choose: Return to Town or Stay Out.
  4. Resolve Town or Stay Out effects.
  5. Resolve Event(s).
  6. Begin next encounter.

Event Frequency

  • Return to Town: resolve 1 Event.
  • Stay Out (first): resolve 2 Events.
  • Stay Out (second consecutive): resolve 3 Events.
  • Stay Out (third consecutive): resolve 4 Events.
  • Scaling resets when the party returns to Town.
  • The starting Event (before Encounter 1) cannot be a Bad Stuff Event.

Restrictions

  • No Event is drawn before the Crypt.
  • Life wording: "lose/gain X life" is current life only. Permanent HP changes must explicitly say "Max HP/Life."

Event Types (Design Layer)

  • 🎁 Reward Events — grant resources, cards, or advantages.
  • ⚠️ Risk Events — force difficult choices.
  • 🔄 Transformation Events — modify decks or game state.
  • 🎲 Chaos Events — high-variance outcomes.

🧿 Trinkets

Trinkets are run modifiers that can be obtained at the start of the game, during the run, or via a Trinket Ticket. The party may hold multiple Trinkets simultaneously — all active Trinkets are in effect.

Selection

  • Present 3 random Trinkets from the pool; players vote on one and it takes effect immediately.
  • Trinket Ticket override: the ticket owner picks 1 of 3 — no table vote.

Ownership

  • Normal Trinkets are party property for the run.
  • A Ticket Trinket is tied to the player who used it; if they leave, the Trinket is removed at the end of the current encounter.
  • If that player leaves mid-encounter, the party may pilot their deck until the encounter ends — the Trinket stays active for that encounter only.

⚔️ Encounter Types

Encounters are chosen randomly. Encounter 1 cannot be Tribal Warfare or Story Fight — reroll if either appears for Encounter 1.

🧠 Master Fight
  • Reveal 3 random Tyrants. Players vote to eliminate 1. Host chooses 1 of the remaining 2.
  • Randomly reveal 3 Commander decks from the Masters pool. Players eliminate 1. Host chooses 1 of the remaining 2 to pilot.
  • The chosen Tyrant's Passive Aura and Signature Move are active for the encounter.
⚔️ Tribal Warfare (+25 XP)
  • Host gets one fewer Affix of the highest-tier Affix for this encounter.
  • Randomly reveal 3 Tribes. Players eliminate 1. Host chooses 1 of the remaining 2 and pilots a deck of that tribe.
  • Host begins the game with the Tribal Warfare Emblem: "At the beginning of your end step, conjure and cast a random creature of the chosen tribe with mana value less than or equal to the number of lands you control."
  • Cannot be rolled for Encounter 1.
📦 Precon Battle
  • Generate 3 random Precon Commander decks. Players eliminate 1. Host chooses 1 of the remaining 2 and pilots it.
  • Note: This encounter type is under review and may be updated in a future version.
🌍 Plane Fight (Experimental) — extra top-tier Affix
  • Randomly reveal 3 Planes. Players eliminate 1. Host chooses 1 of the remaining 2 and pilots a Commander deck themed around that plane.
  • Host gets an additional Affix of the highest tier for this encounter.
📖 Story Fight (Experimental) — one fewer top-tier Affix, +25 XP
  • Host gets one fewer Affix of the highest-tier Affix for this encounter.
  • Reveal 3 Stories. Players eliminate 1. Host chooses 1 of the remaining 2 to pilot.
  • Host starts with 50% additional life (rounded up).
  • This encounter grants an additional +25 XP.
  • Cannot be rolled for Encounter 1.

Phase Transition

Triggers once when the Host's life reaches half or less of their starting life total, or when a player would win the game:

  • Host life becomes half of their starting life total (rounded up).
  • Clear the stack; counter all spells and abilities targeting the Host or permanents they control.
  • The Host and all permanents they control phase out. Remove all damage from their permanents. Host loses all poison counters. Shuffle their graveyard and exile into their library.
  • The current turn continues without the Host. At the beginning of the Host's next turn, they phase in.
  • When the Host phases in, they gain their story's Phase Two bonus (Emblem, Vanguard, or other story ability).
🦹 Villain Fight (Experimental)
  • Reveal 5 random Commanders. Players eliminate 1. Host chooses 1 of the remaining 4.
  • Host builds or spawns a high-power (Bracket 3+) deck around the chosen Commander.
🌪 Wildmagic Fight (Experimental) — no Affixes, +25 XP
  • Host may use any legal deck (Suggested Bracket 3). No Affixes are applied.
  • This encounter grants +25 XP.
  • Host gains the Wild Magic Surge emblem: {0}: Roll a d20 and resolve the corresponding effect. Activate only once each turn.

🔥 Affixes

Affixes are global modifiers applied to the Host at the start of each encounter. They increase difficulty and grant bonus XP per tier. Affixes are rerolled each encounter. If a combination is game-breaking, reroll the highest tier.

Affix Structure — Standard Mode

  • Encounter 1: Tier 1
  • Encounter 2: Tier 1 + Tier 2
  • Encounter 3: Tier 1 + Tier 2 + Tier 3
  • Tier 4 Affixes are not used in Standard mode.

Affix Structure — Variant Mode

  • Encounter 1: Tier 1
  • Encounter 2: Tier 1 + Tier 2
  • Encounter 3: Tier 1 + Tier 2 + Tier 3
  • Encounter 4: Tier 1 + Tier 2 + Tier 3 + Tier 4
🟦 Tier 1 Affixes (+5 XP reward each) — Combat Basics
  • +1/+1 Counters — Host creatures enter the battlefield with a +1/+1 counter.
  • Life Gain — Whenever the Host casts a spell, they gain 1 life.
  • Prismatic — Lands the Host controls have "{T}: Add one mana of any color."
  • Reach — Host creatures have Reach.
  • Scry 1 — At the beginning of the Host's upkeep, they scry 1.
  • Token Generator — At the beginning of the Host's end step, create a 1/1 Servo artifact creature token.
  • Vigilance — Host creatures have Vigilance.
  • Trample — Host creatures have Trample.
🟧 Tier 2 Affixes (+10 XP reward each) — Mid-level Advantages
  • First Strike — Host creatures have First Strike.
  • Ward 1 — Host permanents have Ward {1}.
  • Flying — Host creatures have Flying.
  • Hero Power — The Host gains 1 of 3 random Hero cards.
  • Lifelink — Host creatures have Lifelink.
  • Mana Ramp — At the beginning of the Host's precombat main phase, add {C}.
  • Menace — Host creatures have Menace.
  • Pack Back Up — Each upkeep, open a Mystery Booster and exile one card face down; the Host may cast it.
  • Setup Battle — Before the game begins, reveal cards until a land and a nonland permanent with MV ≤ 4 are found; they enter the battlefield.
🟥 Tier 3 Affixes (+15 XP reward each) — Engine Effects
  • Aether Anomaly — Once each turn when the Host casts a spell, they may cast a random card with the same mana value for free.
  • Cascade — The first spell the Host casts each turn has Cascade.
  • Deathtouch — Host creatures have Deathtouch.
  • Demonstrate — Host spells have Demonstrate.
  • Emblem Battle — The Host gains 1 of 3 random Emblems.
  • Flashback — Instants and sorceries in the Host's graveyard gain Flashback equal to their mana cost.
  • Free Spell — Once per turn, the Host may cast a spell with MV ≤ 3 for free.
  • Rebound — Spells cast from hand are exiled and recast at the Host's next upkeep.
  • Training Grounds — Activated abilities of Host permanents cost {2} less (minimum 1).
  • Twice the Power — Triggered abilities of Host permanents trigger an additional time. (Cannot be paired with Annihilator 1.)
  • Vanguard Battlements — The Host gains 1 of 3 random Vanguards.
  • Vorpal — Whenever one or more creatures the Host controls deal combat damage to a player, that player sacrifices a permanent.
🟪 Tier 4 Affixes (+20 XP reward each) — Run-Defining, Variant Mode Only
  • Annihilator 1 — Non-token creatures the Host controls have Annihilator 1.
  • Arcane Mastery — The first spell the Host casts each turn is copied. Permanent spells create token copies.
  • Double Mana — Lands the Host controls produce double mana.
  • Double Strike — Host creatures have Double Strike.
  • Endless Reinforcements — At end step, create a token copy of a target creature the Host controls (legend rule ignored).
  • Hexproof — Host permanents have Hexproof.
  • Indestructible — Host permanents are Indestructible.
  • Overwhelming Presence — The Host draws an extra card and may play an additional land each turn.
  • Tyrant's Arsenal — At the beginning of the Host's upkeep, create three Treasure tokens.
  • War Preparations — Before the game begins, reveal a land, a creature, and a noncreature permanent; they enter the battlefield.

Doom Rules

  • Doom cards are drawn from the Doom Pool at encounter start. Each is unique unless otherwise specified.
  • The Host may use any number of Doom cards per turn unless otherwise stated.
  • When triggered, resolve the effect immediately then set the card aside — it is removed from the pool for the rest of the encounter.
  • Used Doom cards rejoin the pool at the start of the next encounter.
  • Reroll (once per Host turn): Return any number of Doom cards to the pool and draw the same number of new ones. Rerolled cards are not triggered and may be redrawn. May happen before or after triggering an effect.
💠 Resource / Value Cards
  • I'm Just Getting Started — Add {4}.
  • A Bit of Artificial Insight — Draw two cards. Artifact spells you cast this turn cost {2} less to cast.
  • I Will Witness Your Downfall — Draw four cards.
  • I've Seen What Breaks You — Search your library for a card, put it into your hand, then shuffle.
  • Welcome to My Reality — Choose any number of players. Each chosen player shuffles their hand and graveyard into their library, then draws that many cards. If the Host is not among the chosen players, the Host draws cards until they have seven cards in hand.
🔁 Copy / Spell Manipulation Cards
  • I Can Do That Too! — Copy target noncreature spell. You may choose new targets for the copy.
  • Oooooh, I Like You — Copy target creature spell. You may choose new targets for the copy.
  • No — Counter target spell.
🛡 Defensive / Protection Cards
  • You Are Annoying Me — Choose a player. Creatures that player controls can't attack you or planeswalkers you control this turn. Spells that player controls can't target you or permanents you control this turn.
  • I Am Inevitable — Target permanent gains hexproof and indestructible until end of turn.
  • Minions! Protect Me! — Create a 3/3 black Horror creature token. Prevent all damage that would be dealt to creatures you control this turn.
  • How Do You Like It? — The next time a source of your choice would deal damage to you this turn, prevent that damage. If damage is prevented this way, it deals that much damage to any target instead.
💀 Removal / Control Cards
  • Death Is Inevitable — Destroy up to two target creatures an opponent controls.
  • Death Comes for Us All — Destroy all creatures. They can't be regenerated.
  • You've Outlived Your Usefulness — Exile target nonland permanent.
  • Offer Tribute — Each opponent sacrifices a permanent.
🔥 Damage / Pressure Cards
  • Did That Sting? — Deal 4 damage to each opponent.
  • I'll Show You True Pain — Choose the opponent with the highest life total. That player loses 7 life.
  • My Fury Burns — Until end of turn, if a source you control would deal damage to an opponent or a creature you don't control, it deals that much damage plus 2 instead. Create a 1/1 red Devil creature token with "When this creature dies, it deals 1 damage to any target."
🧬 Disruption Cards
  • Madness Ensues — Each opponent discards two cards.
  • I Need You to Stop — Target player skips their next beginning phase.
🌿 Ramp / Board Development Cards

Host Authority scales with the number of players in the encounter, ensuring the Host can withstand the overwhelming action economy of multiple players. It improves tempo, mana development, card flow, and interaction without removing player counterplay.

💎 Cash Outs & Loot

After a win, resolve XP, Cash Out, and Loot Pool — all three must happen, but there is no required order.

The Host reveals Cash Outs equal to player count + 1. Each player selects one. Unselected Cash Outs are removed from play. Cash Outs are free, tradable, and retained until used.

  • Encounter 2 — 40 XP
  • Encounter 3 — 50 XP

📊 Base XP — Variant Mode (4 Encounters)

  • Encounter 1 — 10 XP
  • Encounter 2 — 20 XP
  • Encounter 3 — 30 XP
  • Encounter 4 — 40 XP

Base XP is per player. Affix bonus XP is added on top (T1 +5, T2 +10, T3 +15, T4 +20 per Affix). Failed encounters grant no XP, no Cash Out, and no Loot Pool.

Cash Out Rules

  • Cash Outs are free to claim.
  • Cash Outs are tradable between players.
  • Cash Outs are retained until used (typically in Town).

Loot Pool Rules

  • Shuffle the defeated deck. Reveal cards equal to player count.
  • Reroll any basic lands revealed.
  • Place revealed cards into a shared Loot Pool. Players may take freely.
  • Cards taken follow all normal deckbuilding rules.

🏘️ Town Buildings

Town is the primary location for deck upgrades and recovery. When the party returns to Town: all players fully heal, use buildings, then resolve 1 Event before the next encounter.

All building use limits reset every time the party enters Town. Town does not use actions — each building is available to all players simultaneously.

🏕️ Stay Out

After an encounter is resolved, players choose to either Return to Town or Stay Out. Staying Out pushes deeper into the dungeon with immediate rewards and increased Event variance.

Stay Out Flow (in order)

  1. Gain XP (base +10 XP; +5 more per consecutive Stay Out)
  2. Gain 2 Mystery Packs per player
  3. Resolve a Supply Drop (roll 1d10)
  4. Resolve a Wanderer
  5. Resolve Events (scaled)
  6. Proceed to next encounter

Scaling Rules

  • XP: +10 first Stay Out, +15 second, +20 third, +25 fourth, etc.
  • Events: 2 Events on first Stay Out; +1 Event each consecutive Stay Out.
  • Mystery Packs: always 2 per player, no scaling.
  • Scaling fully resets when the party returns to Town.
  • Players fully heal at the start of Stay Out.
  • Each pack: keep 1 card (no exceptions unless a specific Traveler grants otherwise).

🕱 Progression to the Crypt

The Crypt is reached by completing all encounter stages in the chosen run mode.

Standard Path

Encounter 1, Encounter 2, Encounter 3, then Crypt.

Variant Path

Encounter 1 through Encounter 4, then Crypt with Tier 4 affixes active in the run.

Each victory increases future difficulty through host scaling. Failed stages still advance the run, skip rewards, and still resolve the post-encounter Town or Stay Out branch.

⚔️ The Crypt

The Crypt is the final encounter of the run, reached after clearing all encounter stages in the chosen mode. No Affixes are applied. The Host always takes the first turn. No Events are resolved.

❌ Losing the Crypt

The run ends. The party does not complete the run. Post-game progression options still apply. Unspent XP converts to Essence.

🏆 Winning the Crypt

The run completes successfully. Gain Essence rewards, Crypt Buff rewards, and any additional progression rewards. Unspent XP converts to Essence.

💀 Death & Departure

Player Death

  • Base rules apply no XP penalty when a player dies.
  • Hosts may apply optional penalties at their discretion (curse, debuff, or custom negative effect) — this is not part of the base ruleset.
  • Dead players return at the end of the encounter.
  • If a player dies during the Crypt, normal death rules apply.

Player Departure

  • If a player departs before the Crypt encounter begins, they do not qualify for Crypt completion rewards.
  • If a player with a Trinket Ticket Trinket departs during an encounter, the party may pilot their deck until the encounter ends; the Trinket is removed at end of that encounter.
  • Players may only Scoop at Sorcery Speed. Full-party encounter concession may be declared at Instant Speed.

🔥 Brands (6)

Brands are permanent progression purchases from the Shop. Brand effects stack and each Brand may be ranked up any number of times — Ranks must be purchased one at a time. Brands are tracked separately from the 4-slot buff inventory — they do not occupy slots. Each additional Rank of the same Brand costs base price × current rank.

All 6 Brands
  • Brand of the Cartographer — 500 Essence base: When drafting, you may replace 2 basic lands with dual lands or triomes.
  • Brand of the Conclave — 1,000 Essence base: During deckbuilding and at The Guild, you get 1 additional Commander choice whenever Commanders are generated for you.
  • Brand of Recurrence — 1,250 Essence base: During deckbuilding and at The Guild, you may reroll Commanders 1 additional time for free. Does not allow rerolling the 100-card nonland draft pile.
  • Brand of the Open Hand — 1,750 Essence base: Whenever you open a pack, that pack contains 1 additional card to choose from.
  • Brand of the Blinded Eye — 2,000 Essence base: During deckbuilding, choose one color to exclude when rolling Commanders.
  • Brand of the Infinite Void — 500 Essence base: Your deck size minimum is reduced by 1 per Rank of this Brand.

💠 Post-Run Shop

Use Essence after runs to buy persistent progression. Tickets, Achievements, Crypt Buffs, and Captures share the 4-slot inventory. Brands are purchased in the Pregame Shop and tracked separately — they do not count against slots.

  • 4 slots total — shared across: Crypt Buffs, Tickets, Achievements, Captures.
  • Standard items: 1 slot each.
  • Vanguard Ticket: 2 slots.
  • Emblem Ticket: 3 slots.
  • Brands: No slot cost. No slot cap. Tracked separately.
