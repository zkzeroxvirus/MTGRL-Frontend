Quick Start

Gameplay Loop

This is the shape of every run — four stages, every session. When you need to find your footing fast, come back here. The detailed sections below cover every edge case and exact ruling.

01

Build your run

Pick your Commander, draft 22 nonlands from your personal 100-card pool, fill in your lands, then slot in any Shop unlocks or persistent progression you're carrying into this run.

02

Fight encounters

Before your first fight: after deckbuilding, players may make one seat-swap adjustment. Then the party resolves a No Bad Stuff Event, then applies Trinket selection. Once Encounter 1 begins, seat swapping is locked. Every encounter is a cooperative Commander game against the Host. Roll a d20 — highest roll picks who goes first.

03

Resolve rewards

Win a fight and you collect all three rewards — XP, a Cash Out, and a card from the Loot Pool. Any order is fine, but all three must happen. Then decide: head to Town or Stay Out in the dungeon.

04

Push to Crypt

Clear every encounter in your chosen run mode and the Crypt opens. This is the final boss. Beat it to earn Essence, Crypt Buffs, and long-term progression that follows you into future runs. The Host always goes first inside.

📜 The Ground Rules

  • Commander legality is always in effect. Your color identity locks your starting deckbuild — but it's not a prison. Cards gained mid-run through Cash Outs, Mystery Packs, and other run rewards can be off-color.
  • This is a cooperative game. One player is the Host, running every encounter and enemy. Everyone else is the Party — you win and lose together.
  • Any progression you've unlocked between runs — Crypt Buffs, Tickets, Achievements, Brands, Captures — is baked in before Encounter 1. It's active from the very first fight.
  • Your 100-card pool is yours alone. If a card is invalid or illegal, replace just that card — the rest of your pool stays exactly as-is.
  • You fully heal before every encounter. No matter what happened last fight, you start the next one at full HP.
  • If you lose a fight, you skip XP, the Cash Out, and the Loot Pool — that's the only penalty. The run keeps going. You still go to Town or Stay Out.
  • Anything involving Scryfall or pack generation follows standard card legality. The Host has final say on any edge cases.

🎮 Host — Know This

  • Standard runs 3 encounters. Variant runs 4 and adds Tier 4 Affixes. Your chosen mode locks in both the encounter count and the Affix ceiling.
  • Your power scales with party size. More players means more Doom cards, a higher Authority level, and higher HP targets at every stage.
  • Roll a d20 before each fight. Whoever rolls highest decides who goes first.

🛡️ Players — Know This

  • XP is earned and spent within a single run — it doesn't carry over. Essence, Brands, Tickets, and other progression are permanent and follow you run after run.
  • Dying doesn't cost you XP. There's no death tax in MTGR.

🚫 Banned and Restricted

Some cards are off the table entirely — and a few more are on a short leash. Everything on this list is always in effect across every MTGR run, unless the Host explicitly says otherwise at the start of the session. One more thing: if you roll a card during deckbuilding that only works in a Draft format — anything with "Draft a card" in its text — reroll it immediately. Those cards have no function here.

🚫 Full Ban — These Cards Don't Exist in MTGR

If one of these shows up during deckbuilding or appears in a pack, treat it like it was never there. Reroll it, replace it, and move on.

  • Show and Tell
  • Silence effects — any effect that stops the Host from casting spells on their own turn (Mandate of Peace is the one exception — it's legal)
  • Collective Voyage
  • Mana Abundance
  • Time Vault
  • Hermit Druid Avatar
  • Braids, Conjurer Adept Avatar
  • Staying Power
  • Rules Lawyer
  • Summoner's Bond
  • Richard Garfield, Ph.D.
  • Double Stroke
  • Sovereign's Realm

⚠️ Restricted — Legal, But You Can't Go Looking for Them

These cards are allowed in MTGR — but you can't hunt them down. They're banned from Scryfall searches and any targeted card-search effect. If one lands in your hand through a random pack open, it's yours to keep and play. You just can't fetch it on purpose.

  • Join Forces (keyword) — cannot be fetched via Scryfall or targeted search
  • Oath of Lieges — cannot be fetched via Scryfall or targeted search
  • Scholarship Sponsor — cannot be fetched via Scryfall or targeted search
  • Zur's Weirding — cannot be fetched via Scryfall or targeted search
  • Tree of Perdition — legal to play, but cannot be combined with Balloon effects under any circumstances

📦 Resources

MTGR runs on two currencies, and knowing the difference between them matters from minute one. One follows you forever across every session. The other burns up at the end of each run — but nothing is ever truly wasted.

✨ Essence

Essence is your long-term investment. It carries across every session and never resets — every point you earn stays yours until you choose to spend it. This is what you use to build your loadout between runs.

  • Earned by completing runs and from end-of-run conversions.
  • On Crypt victory, each eligible player gains 500 Essence.
  • On run end (win or lose), each eligible player gains Essence equal to Deck CMC + Sideboard CMC.
  • Any XP left over when a run ends automatically converts to Essence (win or lose).
  • Spent at the Shop — on pre-game upgrades, Brands, Tickets, Captures, and more.
  • It never resets. Whatever you've saved is yours to keep.

🔶 XP

XP is your fuel for this run and this run only. You earn it while you're in the dungeon, and you spend it while you're still there — on Town buildings, Wanderers, and other mid-run options.

  • Earned from encounter wins, certain Events, and Stay Out bonuses.
  • Spent at Town buildings and with Wanderers while mid-run.
  • Dying doesn't cost you XP — there's no death tax here.
  • When the run ends — win or lose — every unspent XP converts to Essence. Nothing goes to waste.

🛒 Shop, Brands & Inventory

The Shop is where your Essence turns into lasting power. Crypt Buffs and Achievements are earned through play. Tickets and Brands are purchased with Essence. Captures are also bought with Essence and stored for future runs. Almost everything you buy draws from your personal 4-slot inventory — Brands are the one exception. They're permanent, slotless, and active the moment you own them.

🎒 Progression Inventory

Crypt Buffs and Achievements are permanent progression rewards earned through play. Tickets and Captures are permanent progression purchases bought with Essence. During Deckbuilding, you pick which slot-based unlocks you're equipping for this run. The moment Encounter 1 begins, that loadout is locked — no swapping after the first fight starts. You have 4 slots spread across those four item types. Choose the combination that fits your build.

  • 🛡️ Crypt Buffs — Earned from Crypt wins. 1 slot each.
  • 🎟️ Tickets — Purchased with Essence. Standard 1 slot · Vanguard 2 slots · Emblem 3 slots.
  • 🏆 Achievements — Earned by meeting milestone conditions in play. 1 slot each.
  • 🔒 Captures — Purchased with Essence to save cards for future runs. 1 slot each.
Selected during Deckbuilding Locked in before Encounter 1 4 slots per player Brands are always active — no slots, no lock-in

✨ Brand Ranking

You can rank a Brand up as many times as you want — but each Rank is purchased individually, one at a time. Every additional Rank costs the Brand's base price × its current rank. The higher you push a Brand, the more powerful it gets — each Rank stacks its effect on top of the last.

🗓️ When Can You Spend?

  • Pre-game — From the start of deckbuilding until Encounter 1 begins. Your window for Commander upgrades and deckbuilding options.
  • Tickets, Brands, and Captures — These are Essence purchases, not earned rewards.
  • Crypt Buffs and Achievements — These are earned through gameplay, not bought from the Shop.
💰 Pre-Game Purchases — Spend Before the First Fight

These options are open from the start of deckbuilding until Encounter 1 begins. Once the first fight starts, this window is gone.

  • 0 Essence — Commander Gamble: Mulligan your Commander for 1 new option. You must take it. (If it is absolutely unplayable, the Host may allow a re-roll.) Your next Guild purchase this run is free.
  • 0 Essence — Partner / Background / etc.: Choose a Partner, Background, or similar pairing. If it says "Partner with X," you gain X instead.
🔥 Brands (6) — Permanent, Slotless, Cross-Run

Brands are permanent upgrades that follow you into every run forever. They don't take up inventory slots, they don't need to be equipped at deckbuilding, and they never expire. The moment you own one, it's active. Rank them up to amplify the effect.

  • Brand of the Cartographer — 500 Essence base: When drafting, you may replace 2 basic lands with dual lands or triomes.
  • Brand of the Conclave — 1,000 Essence base: During deckbuilding and at The Guild, you get 1 additional Commander choice whenever Commanders are generated for you.
  • Brand of Recurrence — 1,250 Essence base: During deckbuilding and at The Guild, you may reroll Commanders 1 additional time for free. Does not allow rerolling the 100-card nonland draft pile.
  • Brand of the Open Hand — 1,750 Essence base: Whenever you open a pack, that pack contains 1 additional card to choose from.
  • Brand of the Blinded Eye — 2,000 Essence base: During deckbuilding, choose one color to exclude when rolling Commanders.
  • Brand of the Infinite Void — 500 Essence base: Your deck size minimum is reduced by 1 per Rank of this Brand.

These are the progression items you buy with Essence. Tickets and Captures use inventory slots and carry into future runs. Brands are purchased with Essence too, but are tracked separately and do not use slots. If Crypt completion awards a Buff you already have unlocked, that duplicate is immediately sold for +250 Essence.

  • 250 Essence — Sell Buffs: Sell a Crypt Buff back to the Host in exchange for 250 Essence. Duplicate Buffs awarded from Crypt completion are auto-sold for +250 Essence immediately.
  • Capture Non-Commander (base 500 Essence, 1 slot): Save 1 card from your deck to your collection. Captured cards may be used in future deck builds but count against your 39 (no free allocation). Cost increases by 250 Essence per additional capture: 1st = 500, 2nd = 750, 3rd = 1,000, and so on.

🎴 Deckbuilding

Every run starts here. These five steps happen in order — no skipping, no shortcuts. Your starting deckbuild is locked to your Commander's color identity, but that restriction loosens as the run goes on. Off-color cards can enter your deck through Cash Outs, Mystery Packs, and other mid-run sources.

01

Choose Your Commander

You get 5 random legal Commanders to start. Don't like the options? You have up to 4 free mulligans to reroll the full set. When you're ready, pick one and commit.

02

Draft Your Cards

Generate your personal 100-card pool within your color identity, then pick 22 cards from it. Non-basic lands can show up in the pool and are fair game to draft. The pool is fixed — you can't reroll it whole. If one specific card is invalid, replace just that card.

03

Add Lands

Add 16 total lands. Dual count by colors: 2 colors = 2 duals, 3 = 4, 4 = 6, 5 = 8. Fill the rest with basics.

04

Free Pick

You get one free card of your choice — anything Commander-legal within your color identity, as long as it's not Sol Ring or a Gamechanger. Pick it, then stamp it with a Scryfall decal so everyone knows which card it is.

05

Apply Progression

Choose which persistent unlocks go into your 4 progression slots for this run — Tickets, Brands, and any Shop purchases. Lock in your four picks, then confirm you're at Commander + 39 minimum. That's your run deck.

Deck Size — What Changes the Floor

  • Standard start: Commander + 39 is the minimum. 40 cards total.
  • Can start larger: Certain Tickets — Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, Trinket, and others — add cards that sit outside your 39 allocation. Your deck can legally start with more than 40 because of these.
  • Can drop below mid-run: Brand of the Infinite Void lowers the floor by 1 per Rank. Stack it enough and your deck can legally shrink below its starting count as the run progresses.

Hard Rules — Don't Skip These

  • Color identity governs your starting deckbuild. The only way to include off-color cards at this stage is through a progression effect that explicitly grants it.
  • You cannot reroll your 100-card pool as a whole. One invalid card? Replace that card alone.
  • Stickers, Attractions, and Contraptions don't belong in your pool. If one appears, reroll and replace it immediately.
  • Your free pick can be anything Commander-legal in your color identity — except Sol Ring, Gamechangers, and Commander-banned cards.
  • No two-card infinite or game-ending combos allowed — in any deck at the table, yours or the Host's.
  • Your Scryfall-decal card specifically cannot be part of a three-card combo.

🎲 Gameplay Loop

Here's the shape of every run. You fight encounters, make a rest choice in between, and keep pushing until the Crypt opens. That's the loop — and it repeats every session.

01

Before Encounter 1

Before the first fight kicks off, after deckbuilding the party may make one seat-swap adjustment. Then the party resolves a No Bad Stuff Event — a safe intro event to get things rolling. Then the party reveals 3 Trinkets and picks one together. Once Encounter 1 begins, seat swapping is no longer allowed.

02

Encounter

The Host draws a random encounter and resolves any setup it requires. Once setup is done, both sides roll a d20 — whoever rolls higher gets to choose turn order.

03

Victory — Resolve Rewards

Win the fight and you earn all three rewards — resolve them in any order: XP (mode table + Affix bonuses), your Cash Out, and the Loot Pool.

03

Defeat — No Rewards

Lose the fight and you walk away empty — no XP, no Cash Out, no Loot Pool. That's the only penalty. The run keeps going.

04

Between Encounters

After every encounter — win or lose — the party picks their next rest: 🏘️ Town or 🏕️ Stay Out. Resolve those effects, then loop back to the next fight.

05

Crypt

Clear every encounter in your chosen run mode and the Crypt opens. The Host always goes first inside — no roll, no debate. On victory, each eligible player gains 500 Essence and the awarded Crypt Buff (duplicate Buffs are immediately sold for +250 Essence). On run end (win or lose), each eligible player also gains Essence equal to Deck CMC + Sideboard CMC, and unspent XP converts to Essence.

🏘️ Town vs 🏕️ Stay Out

🏘️ Town

Town is your safe harbor. Everyone heals, you get access to the buildings, and you resolve one Event before the next fight.

  • All players fully heal
  • Use Town buildings (limited uses per visit; all reset on entry)
  • Resolve 1 Event before the next encounter

Best for: controlled upgrades, building synergies, recovering safely.

🏕️ Stay Out

Stay Out is the high-risk, high-reward route. You still heal, but skip the buildings in exchange for XP, Mystery Packs, and extra Events — and it keeps stacking the longer you stay out.

  • All players fully heal
  • Each player gains +10 XP (scales with consecutive Stay Outs)
  • Each player opens 2 Mystery Packs
  • Resolve a Supply Drop
  • Resolve a Wanderer
  • Resolve 2 Events (instead of 1; each additional consecutive Stay Out adds +1 more)

Best for: maximizing XP and cards, high-variance high-reward play.

Death & Scoop Rules

Dying isn't the end of your run — just this fight.

  • You die, you're out for this encounter — but you come back for the next one.
  • Dying costs you nothing — no XP penalty.
  • If a player has to leave mid-fight, the rest of the party can pilot their deck until that encounter ends.
  • Scooping individually? Sorcery speed only.
  • The whole party conceding the encounter? That can happen at Instant speed.

📘 Turn Structure (Party vs Host)

Both sides play with standard MTG turn structure — but the party shares theirs.

🛡️ Party Turn

The whole party acts as one. You share a single turn — one untap, one upkeep, one draw, main phase, combat, second main, and end step. Coordinate with your teammates on sequencing and priority. You're one unit.

👹 Host Turn

The Host plays their turn normally, following whatever setup and scaling rules apply to that encounter.

Extra turns belong to whoever created them — they don't pass to the rest of the party unless a rule specifically says otherwise.

Teaming & Hidden Hand Information (Table Policy)
  • Base table default: No teams are used.
  • Hand privacy: Players do not look at allied players' hands unless a card/effect instructs a reveal.
  • Host variants: Regular Host tables may allow team hand-sharing only if declared up front as a custom table rule.

🎲 Events

Events are the wild cards between encounters — they can reward you, punish you, shake up the game state, or just make things weird. They trigger after your rest choice and finish before the next fight starts. One at a time, in order, and each one has to fully resolve before the next one begins.

When Events Happen

  1. Encounter is defeated.
  2. Resolve your rewards.
  3. Choose: Town or Stay Out.
  4. Resolve Town or Stay Out effects.
  5. Resolve your Event(s).
  6. Begin the next encounter.

How Many Events You Get

  • Town: 1 Event.
  • Stay Out (1st consecutive): 2 Events.
  • Stay Out (2nd consecutive): 3 Events.
  • Stay Out (3rd consecutive): 4 Events.
  • The count resets when you return to Town.
  • The very first Event of the run (before Encounter 1) can't be a Bad Stuff Event.
  • After deckbuilding and before Encounter 1 begins, players may make one seat-swap adjustment; once Encounter 1 begins, seat swapping is no longer allowed.

Restrictions

  • No Event is drawn immediately before the Crypt.
  • When an Event says "lose/gain X life" — that's current life only. Anything that changes your Max HP has to say "Max HP" or "Max Life" explicitly.

Event Types

  • 🎁 Reward Events — resources, cards, and advantages.
  • ⚠️ Risk Events — tough choices with real consequences.
  • 🔄 Transformation Events — alter your deck or the game state.
  • 🎲 Chaos Events — high-variance, anything-goes outcomes.

🧿 Trinkets

Trinkets are passive run modifiers that change how encounters play out. You can pick one up at the start of the run, find them during it, or use a Trinket Ticket to grab one on demand. Trinket Ticket ownership and slot usage are tracked per player, not as one shared party slot.

How You Pick One

  • 3 random Trinkets are revealed from the pool. The party votes, one is chosen, and it takes effect immediately.
  • Trinket Ticket override: if you have a Ticket, you pick 1 of the 3 yourself — no vote needed.
  • Ticket limits are per player. This does not cap the whole party at one total Trinket.

Who Owns It

  • Trinkets picked by the party belong to everyone for the whole run.
  • A Ticket Trinket is tied to the player who used it. If they leave the run, it's removed at the end of the current encounter.
  • If they leave mid-fight, the party can pilot their deck until that encounter ends — the Trinket stays active for that fight only.

Every encounter is drawn randomly — you never know what's coming next. The one exception: Encounter 1 can't be Tribal Warfare or Story Fight. If either shows up on that first roll, reroll it.

The standard format. You're facing a Tyrant-powered Commander, and the party gets a say in shaping who shows up.

  • 3 random Tyrants are revealed. The party votes to eliminate one, then the Host picks from the remaining two.
  • 3 Commander decks from the Masters pool are revealed. The party eliminates one, and the Host picks one to pilot.
  • The chosen Tyrant's Passive Aura and Signature Move are both active for this encounter.

The Host runs a tribal deck and gets a passive creature engine to match. You earn +25 XP for fighting through it.

  • The Host gets one fewer top-tier Affix for this fight.
  • 3 Tribes are revealed. The party eliminates one, and the Host picks one to pilot a deck around.
  • Host begins the game with the Tribal Warfare Emblem: "At the beginning of your end step, conjure and cast a random creature of the chosen tribe with mana value less than or equal to the number of lands you control."

📦 Precon Battle

A precon vs. the party — straightforward format, still being refined.

  • 3 random Precon Commander decks are generated. The party eliminates one, and the Host pilots one of the remaining two.
  • Note: This encounter type is under review and may be updated in a future version.

📖 Story Fight (Experimental) — one fewer top-tier Affix, +25 XP

A narrative encounter with a twist: the Host comes back. When they hit half health, Phase Transition fires and the fight enters round two.

  • The Host gets one fewer top-tier Affix for this fight.
  • 3 Stories are revealed. The party eliminates one, and the Host pilots one of the remaining two.
  • The Host starts with 50% additional life (rounded up).
  • This encounter grants an additional +25 XP.
  • Can't be rolled for Encounter 1.

Phase Transition

When the Host drops to half their starting life — or when a player would win the game — this triggers. Once only.

  • Host life becomes half of their starting life total (rounded up).
  • Clear the stack; counter all spells and abilities targeting the Host or permanents they control.
  • The Host and all permanents they control phase out. Remove all damage from their permanents. Host loses all poison counters. Shuffle their graveyard and exile into their library.
  • The current turn continues without the Host. At the beginning of the Host's next turn, they phase in.
  • When the Host phases in, they gain their story's Phase Two bonus (Emblem, Vanguard, or other story ability).

How Doom Works

  • Doom cards are drawn fresh at the start of each encounter. Each one is unique unless the card says otherwise.
  • Doom cards are tracked in a separate Doom hand/zone, not the Host's normal MTG hand.
  • Doom cards do not count toward hand size limits.
  • The Host can trigger any number of Doom cards per turn — no limit unless a card specifies one.
  • When a Doom card triggers, resolve it and set it aside. It's out of the pool for the rest of this encounter.
  • Set-aside Doom cards rejoin the pool at the start of the next encounter.
  • Reroll (once per Host turn): The Host can return any Doom cards from their Doom hand to the pool and draw the same number of new ones. Rerolled cards aren't triggered and can be redrawn. This can happen before or after triggering an effect.

🏘️ Town Buildings

Every time you make it back to Town, everyone fully heals. All buildings are open to the whole party at once — no turn order, no actions, just use what you need. After buildings, you resolve 1 Event, then you're back out.

Building limits reset every visit. This is also when Cash Outs can be traded between players.

🏦 Bank — 1 use per Town

Swap cards between your deck and sideboard. Move up to 6 cards in any direction — you can move all 6 one way, split them, whatever you need. Bank interactions can only happen while in Town.

🛍️ Bazaar — 2 Deals per Town + unlimited Cashout Sales

Two things happen here — trading and selling. You get 2 Deals per visit, plus you can sell Cash Outs for XP as many times as you want. The two functions are completely separate.

Deals (2 uses per Town): Each deal is one of the following exchanges:

  • Card ↔ Card
  • Card ↔ Essence (2× the card's mana value)
  • Cashout ↔ Cashout
  • Cashout ↔ Card

Cashout Sale (free, unlimited): Sell any number of Cashouts to the Host for 10 XP each. Cashout Sales do not consume a Deal usage and may be done any number of times.

⛪ Cathedral — 1 use per Town

Describe a card, and the Host finds a real, legal card that matches — you can't name a specific card, just the shape of it. Once you have it, you choose what to do with it.

  • Add the card to your deck, or
  • Destroy it and gain Essence equal to 2× its mana value
⚔️ The Guild — Unlimited (10 XP + d6)

Want a different Commander? Declare a color combination before rolling, pay 10 XP, then roll a d6. You get (roll + 2) random legal Commanders in those colors — pick one or keep what you have. Your old Commander drops into your deck or sideboard.

If your new Commander changes your color identity, adjust your deck immediately to stay legal.

🔮 Mystic — Unlimited (5 XP)

Cut dead weight and convert it. Pay 5 XP, destroy a nonland card from your deck, and gain Essence equal to twice its mana value. Good for trimming cards you've outgrown and turning them into something useful.

🌀 Portal — 1 use per Town (up to 15 XP)

Spend XP to conjure a pack of Temporal cards — the more you spend, the bigger the pack. Use a type or keyword to shape what you get. These aren't permanent cards, but they're real enough to play.

Temporal card rules: Temporal cards must stay in your deck. They can't be traded, sideboarded, or sacrificed to Town or Traveler effects. They vanish permanently at the end of the encounter in which they were used.

🍺 Tavern — 1 use per Town

Tune your mana base. Pick one of two options — bring in basics, or trade basics for duals. One use per visit, so pick the option that helps you most right now.

  • Option A: Remove 0–2 cards from your deck and add 0–4 basic lands of your choice.
  • Option B: Remove 3–5 basic lands and add 0–2 dual lands of your choice.

Tavern is for deck smoothing and mana fixing.

🏕️ Stay Out

After every encounter you choose: go back to Town, or stay out and keep pushing. Staying Out means more XP, more packs, a Supply Drop, and a Wanderer — but also more Events before your next fight. The rewards scale the longer you stay out consecutively.

Resolution

  1. Gain XP (base +10 XP; +5 more per consecutive Stay Out)
  2. Gain 2 Mystery Packs per player
  3. Resolve a Supply Drop (roll 1d10)
  4. Resolve a Wanderer
  5. Resolve Events (scaled)
  6. Proceed to next encounter

Scaling Rules

  • XP: +10 first Stay Out, +15 second, +20 third, +25 fourth, etc.
  • Events: 2 Events on first Stay Out; +1 Event each consecutive Stay Out.
  • Mystery Packs: always 2 per player, no scaling.
  • Scaling fully resets when the party returns to Town.
  • Players fully heal at the start of Stay Out.
  • Each pack: keep 1 card (no exceptions unless a specific Traveler grants otherwise).

⚰️ The Crypt & Endgame

Every run ends the same way — the Crypt. Clear all the encounters, then face it. Win and you walk away with everything. Lose and the run ends, but progression still counts.

🕱 Path to the Crypt

You reach the Crypt by clearing every encounter in your chosen run mode.

Standard Path

Encounter 1, Encounter 2, Encounter 3, then Crypt.

Variant Path

Encounter 1 through Encounter 4, then Crypt with Tier 4 affixes active in the run.

Every encounter — win or lose — scales the Host harder going forward. Lose a fight and the run still continues, you just skip the rewards and head to your Town or Stay Out choice.

⚔️ The Crypt

No Affixes. No Events. The Host always goes first. This is the fight everything else was building toward.

❌ Losing the Crypt

The run ends. You do not get win-only completion rewards, but post-game progression still applies: each eligible player gains Essence equal to Deck CMC + Sideboard CMC, and unspent XP converts to Essence.

🏆 Winning the Crypt

Run complete. Each eligible player gains 500 Essence and receives the awarded Crypt Buff. If that Buff is already unlocked, it is immediately sold for +250 Essence. Run-end progression still applies: each eligible player gains Essence equal to Deck CMC + Sideboard CMC, and unspent XP converts to Essence.

On either outcome, each eligible player gains Deck CMC + Sideboard CMC as Essence, and unspent XP converts to Essence at run end.

💀 Death & Departure

Player Death

  • Dying doesn't cost you XP — that's the base rule.
  • The Host can add optional penalties like curses or debuffs if they want — but that's not standard, it's their call.
  • You come back when the encounter ends.
  • Normal death rules apply in the Crypt too.

Player Departure

  • Leave before the Crypt starts and you don't get completion rewards.
  • Leave mid-encounter with a Trinket Ticket Trinket and the party can pilot your deck until the fight ends — the Trinket gets removed after.
  • You can only Scoop at Sorcery Speed. The full party can concede the whole encounter at Instant Speed.

If a Ticket reveals Vanguards, Trinkets, Conspiracies, or Emblems, you may decline the offered options and keep the Ticket for future sessions, but you only see that Ticket type's options once per session unless you gain another Ticket of that type from a Cashout.

Ticket Resolution Rules
