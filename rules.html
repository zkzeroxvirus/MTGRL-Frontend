Quick Start

Run Flow at a Glance

Use this section when you need the overall structure fast. The detailed rules below stay authoritative for edge cases and exact limits.

01

Build the run

Choose a commander, draft 22 nonlands from your own 100-card pool, add lands, and apply any pregame shop or progression effects.

02

Fight encounters

Resolve Event, then Trinket before encounter 1, then fight encounters with shared party sequencing and d20 turn-order rolls.

03

Resolve rewards

On a win, resolve XP, Cash Out, and Loot Pool in that order, then choose Town or Stay Out before Events.

04

Push to Crypt

Clear every encounter in the chosen mode, then beat the Crypt for Essence, Crypt Buffs, and long-term progression.

Host reminders

  • Commander legality and color identity always apply.
  • Mode determines encounter count and whether Tier 4 affixes can appear.
  • Host scaling uses player count, encounter stage, affix tier count, Doom, and Authority.
  • On a failed encounter, skip rewards only, then still proceed to Town or Stay Out.
  • High roll on the pre-fight d20 chooses turn order.

Player reminders

  • XP is only for the current run. Essence, Brands, Tickets, and other progression persist.
  • Failed encounters skip XP, Cash Out, and Loot Pool only.
  • Base rules apply no XP penalty when a player dies.

📜 Global Rules

  • Commander legality applies throughout the run: commander, deck cards, and later additions must remain legal and within color identity.
  • The party plays cooperatively as one team while the Host controls encounters and opposing forces.
  • Persistent progression is applied before encounter 1: Crypt Buffs, Tickets, Achievements, Brands, and Captures.
  • Each player generates their own 100-card pool (any card type except basic lands); once generated, only invalid or illegal cards are replaced.
  • After each encounter stage, players reset to full HP before the next branch resolves.
  • Failed encounters are not replayed and skip rewards only (XP, Cash Out, Loot Pool), then still proceed to Town or Stay Out and Events.
  • Scryfall-based effects and pack generation still follow normal legality and Host adjudication.

🚫 Banned and Restricted

These constraints apply across MTGR runs unless the Host explicitly overrides them.

Fully Banned Highlights

  • Show and Tell
  • Silence effects that stop the Host from casting spells on their turn (except Mandate of Peace)
  • Collective Voyage, Time Vault, and other listed MTGR full-ban cards
  • Cards with Draft-style gameplay text are rerolled during deckbuilding

Restricted Highlights

  • Join Forces effects are excluded from Scryfall or targeted search sourcing
  • Oath of Lieges and Scholarship Sponsor are excluded from targeted sourcing
  • Tree of Perdition cannot be used with Balloon interactions
  • Use Host adjudication if an effect conflicts with legality constraints

📦 Player Limits

Limits apply per player unless a rule explicitly says the party shares a resource or choice.

Required

1 commander, 39-card library, and a legal deck after every swap, reward, or Guild change.

Inventory Slots

4 shared progression slots across Crypt Buffs, Tickets, Achievements, and Captures. Brands are tracked separately.

Ticket Slot Costs

Most tickets cost 1 slot. Vanguard costs 2 and Emblem costs 3. Trinkets remain capped at one active trinket at a time.

📦 Resources

✨ Essence

Essence is the long-term resource. It persists between sessions and powers pre-game and post-game actions. Spend it outside the run to unlock options.

🔶 XP

XP is your in-run currency. Spend it on Town buildings and Wanderers. Unspent XP disappears when the run ends, but player death no longer causes a base XP penalty.

🎒 Progression Inventory

Crypt Buffs, Tickets, Achievements, and Captures use the 4-slot inventory. Brands sit outside that cap and can be ranked up repeatedly.

🛒 Pregame Shop (Essence)

These one-run purchases are made before encounter 1 during deckbuilding.

  • 🎲 0 Essence — Commander Gamble: mulligan your commander for 1 new option and your next Guild purchase this run is free.
  • 👥 0 Essence — Partner or Background etc.: choose a Partner, Background, or similar option. If it says Partner with X, you gain X.
  • 🌍 35 Essence — Land Replacement: replace a basic land with a legal dual land or triome. Repeatable.
  • 🔁 50 Essence — Commander Mulligan: delete your current 5 commander options and generate 5 new legal choices. Repeatable.
  • 🧾 Current Pregame Shop focuses on commander shaping and mana fixing. Persistent tickets now cover effects like free Leylines and other long-term unlocks.

🎴 Deckbuilding

All cards must remain Commander-legal and fit the current commander's color identity.

  1. Generate 5 random legal commanders, choose 1, and use up to 4 free commander mulligans before encounter 1 if needed.
  2. Generate your own 100-card pool in your color identity (any card type except basic lands), then draft 22 cards from that pool. The pool cannot be rerolled as a whole.
  3. If an individual card is invalid or illegal for play, reroll and replace only that card.
  4. Add 16 total lands. Two colors add 2 duals, three colors add 4 duals, four colors add 6 duals, five colors add 8 duals. Fill the rest with basics.
  5. Add 1 free-choice card that is Commander legal, fits color identity, and is not Sol Ring or a Gamechanger.
  6. Finalize persistent progression, confirm the deck is Commander plus 39, then resolve pre-first-encounter setup in order: Event, then Trinket (starting Event cannot be Bad Stuff).

🎲 Gameplay Loop

The loop is encounter, rewards, branch choice, Events, then the next encounter until the Crypt.

Before Encounter 1

Resolve Event first (starting Event cannot be Bad Stuff), then resolve Trinket using the Trinket System flow.

Encounter

Host resolves a random encounter. Both sides roll a d20 and the high roll chooses turn order.

Victory Path

Resolve rewards in strict order: XP, Cash Out, then Loot Pool. After that choose Town or Stay Out, then resolve Event(s).

Failure Path

Failed encounters grant no XP, no Cash Out, and no Loot Pool. Then the party still proceeds through Town or Stay Out, resolves Events, and advances.

🏘️ Town

Fully heal, use Town buildings, then resolve 1 Event before the next encounter.

🏕️ Staying Out

Fully heal, gain XP, open 2 Mystery Packs per player, resolve a Supply Drop, resolve a Wanderer, then resolve scaled Event draws. Consecutive Stay Outs add +5 XP each time and +1 additional Event each time.

Base rules apply no XP penalty when a player dies. Dead players return in later encounter flow.

Scoop timing: individual scoops are sorcery speed only; full-party encounter concession may be declared at instant speed.

📘 Turn Structure (Party vs Host)

🛡️ Party Turn

All players act as one. One untap, upkeep, draw, main phase, combat, main phase 2, and end step. Coordinate sequencing and priority as a shared team.

👹 Host Turn

Host plays normally under the encounter's setup and scaling rules.

Extra turns belong only to the player who created them unless another rule explicitly overrides that.

In the Crypt, the Host always starts first.

🏘️ Town Buildings

Town no longer uses actions. Buildings are either limited-use per Town visit or unlimited-use as long as their XP costs are paid.

All building limits reset every time the party returns to Town.

Limited-Use Buildings

  • Bank — 1 use per Town
  • Bazaar — 2 uses per Town
  • Cathedral — 1 use per Town
  • Portal — 1 use per Town
  • Tavern — 1 use per Town

Unlimited Buildings

  • Blacksmith — Upgrade 25 XP or Augment 50 XP
  • The Guild — 10 XP plus d6 commander change
  • Merchant — paid packs with scaling precision
  • Mystic — 5 XP to prune a nonland for Essence
Building Rules
  • 🏦 Bank — move up to 6 cards between deck and sideboard.
  • ⚒️ Blacksmith — Upgrade changes stats, keywords, or costs. Augment adds or changes rules text. A card can receive only 1 Blacksmith modification.
  • 🛍️ Bazaar — make deals such as card for card, card for Essence, Cash Out trades, or Cash Out to Host for 10 XP.
  • ⛪ Cathedral — describe a card request, then either add the provided card to your deck or destroy it for Essence equal to twice its mana value.
  • 🏰 The Guild — choose a color combination, pay 10 XP plus a d6 roll, then receive roll + 2 legal commanders in those colors and optionally swap commanders. Your deck must be fixed immediately if legality changes.
  • 🛒 Merchant — buy 15-card packs: Mystery 5 XP, ID 10 XP, Pro 20 XP, Mythic 50 XP, OTAG 75 XP. Keep 1 card from each pack unless an explicit Traveler effect says otherwise.
  • 🔮 Mystic — pay 5 XP, destroy a nonland card, and gain Essence equal to twice its mana value.
  • 🌀 Portal — spend up to 15 XP to generate a type- or keyword-based Temporal pack with size equal to XP spent. Temporal cards cannot be traded, sideboarded, or sacrificed to Town/Traveler effects.
  • 🍻 Tavern — either trade deck cards for basics, or swap 3 to 5 basics into up to 2 dual lands for mana smoothing.

🌟 Travelers & Wanderers

Town Travelers and Stay Out Wanderers are now separate systems with different timing and power level.

Town Travelers

Travelers appear when the party goes to Town and offer optional bonuses layered on top of the current building system.

Current Town traveler pool includes Bearded Grunt, Bullywug Royal, Card Copier, Card-Eating Ogre, Cats in a Coat, Centaur Scouter, Centepoid Warrior, Commander Mimic, Dwarven Artificer, Elven Demonologist, Fungal Lich, Giff Bandito, Giant Ice Toad, Hell's Librarian, Hollyphant, Kimi the Cat, Silly the Jester, The Trader, Vanguard Mercenaries, Wandering Card Merchant, and Zorbo.

Stay Out Wanderers

When the party Stays Out, reveal 1 random Wanderer. Each player may interact with that Wanderer once by paying XP.

Wanderers are lower impact than Town buildings and focus on deck smoothing, minor upgrades, and resource exchange.

Traveler Highlights
  • Bearded Grunt can grant XP, more building access, or extra basics.
  • Commander Mimic and The Guild effects now matter together because commander-swapping is a full Town strategy.
  • Silly the Jester, Wandering Card Merchant, and Hollyphant all extend Town value without replacing core building rules.
  • Wanderers never replace Town buildings; they are a separate Stay Out reward step.

💎 Cash Outs & Loot

After a win, rewards resolve in this order: XP, Cash Out, then Loot Pool.

Cash Outs are free, tradable, and retained until used. Loot Pool cards come directly from the defeated encounter deck.

Cash Out Pool Snapshot
  • Pack rewards include Alpha, Artifact, Enchantment, ID, Mono, Mystery, Mythic, Planeswalker, and Utility Land packs.
  • Free Town service cash outs include Bonus Bank, Bonus Cathedral, Bonus Mystic, Bonus Tavern, Bonus Bazaar, and Free Guild.
  • Blacksmith service cash outs include Free Upgrade and Free Augment.
  • The Host reveals Cash Outs equal to player count, then each player selects one unless another rule says otherwise.
  • Loot Pool reveals cards equal to player count from the defeated deck, rerolling basic lands.

🧙 Events & Run Modes

Encounter count, base XP, and the affix ceiling are now defined by the selected run mode.

Mode Encounter Count Base XP By Stage Affix Ceiling
Standard 3 encounters, then Crypt 30 / 40 / 50 Tier 1-3 only
Variant 4 encounters, then Crypt 10 / 20 / 30 / 40 Tier 1-4
Event Timing
  • Town path resolves 1 Event after building usage.
  • Stay Out resolves 2 Events on the first consecutive Stay Out, then adds +1 Event for each additional consecutive Stay Out.
  • Events happen outside combat, resolve one at a time, and do not occur during the Crypt.
  • After a failed encounter, the party still resolves Town or Stay Out and Event timing normally.
Scaling Notes
  • Host HP, affix tier count, Doom, and Authority scale by player count and encounter stage.
  • Stay Out scaling resets only when the party returns to Town.
  • The reward system stays fast by keeping XP predictable and letting Cash Out and Loot Pool carry the variance.

🔥 Host Scaling & Affixes

Affixes are tiered Host modifiers that increase difficulty and add bonus XP to the encounter reward.

Tier bonus XP is +5, +10, +15, and +20 for Tiers 1 through 4.

🟦 Tier 1 Affixes (+5 XP reward)
  • +1/+1 Counters, Life Gain, Prismatic, Reach, Scry 1, Token Generator, Vigilance, Trample, Ward 1
🟧 Tier 2 Affixes (+10 XP reward)
  • First Strike, Flying, Hero Power, Lifelink, Mana Ramp, Menace, Pack Back Up, Setup Battle
🟥 Tier 3 Affixes (+15 XP reward)
  • Aether Anomaly, Cascade, Deathtouch, Demonstrate, Emblem Battle, Flashback, Free Spell, Rebound, Training Grounds, Twice the Power, Vanguard Battlements, Vorpal
🟪 Tier 4 Affixes (+20 XP reward)
  • Annihilator 1, Arcane Mastery, Double Mana, Double Strike, Endless Reinforcements, Hexproof, Indestructible, Overwhelming Presence, Tyrant's Arsenal, War Preparations

🕱 Progression to the Crypt

You no longer reach the Crypt by hitting an HP threshold. You reach it by completing all encounter stages in the chosen run mode.

Standard Path

Encounter 1, Encounter 2, Encounter 3, then Crypt.

Variant Path

Encounter 1 through Encounter 4, then Crypt with Tier 4 affixes active in the run.

Each victory increases future difficulty through host scaling. Failed stages still advance the run, skip rewards, and still resolve the post-encounter Town or Stay Out branch.

🧿 Trinket System

Trinkets are a pre-first-encounter run modifier resolved after the starting Event step.

Because several individual Trinkets changed across rules revisions, this page now tracks the current selection flow instead of an old itemized list.

Selection Rules
  • Standard flow: present 3 random Trinkets, then the table votes on 1.
  • Trinket Ticket override: the ticket owner chooses 1 of 3 random Trinkets instead of the table vote.
  • Only 1 Trinket may be active at a time unless a separate rule explicitly says otherwise.
  • Normal Trinkets are party property for the run. A Trinket created by a player's ticket is tied to that player and leaves when that player leaves.
  • Pre-first-encounter setup resolves both in order: Event, then Trinket.

⚔️ The Crypt

Entering

Reached after all encounter stages in the chosen mode are complete. The Host pulls the final boss.

Rules

The Crypt is the final encounter, uses its own boss setup, and always takes the first turn.

Crypt fights are significantly harder than prior stages and decide the run outright.

If the party loses to the Crypt, the run ends. If the party wins, the run completes successfully.

❌ Losing

The run ends and normal post-game progression options still matter, but the party does not complete the run.

🏆 Winning

Gain Essence rewards, Crypt Buff rewards, and additional progression rewards defined by the active systems.

✨ Season Progression

Season 1 progression is now organized into Crypt Buffs, Tickets, Brands, Achievements, and Captures.

Crypt Buffs

There are currently 14 Season 1 Crypt Buffs. Examples include Quick Spell, Spiritual Guidance, Respited Gift, Treasure Pirate, and Flame of Progress.

Achievement Buckets

Current achievement groups include General Achievements, Creature-Type Achievements, and Guild Identity Achievements.

Captures

Capture Non-Commander starts at 500 Essence and increases by 250 for each additional capture. Each capture uses 1 slot.

Current Achievement Examples
  • Victory Lap — 2 additional free mulligans after beating 3 Crypt bosses in one session.
  • One with Death — a second free card during deck creation, with one allowed to be a Game Changer.
  • Dog's Best Friend — choose 1 of 3 Companions at deckbuilding if you earned the unlock.
  • Nature's Blessing — swap dual-land gain for triomes after winning a Crypt with 50 or more lands.
  • Adaptive Pattern, Harmony's Bloom, and the other guild buffs are now grouped under Guild Identity Achievements.

💠 Progression Shop

Use Essence before or after runs to buy persistent progression. Tickets, Achievements, Crypt Buffs, and Captures share the 4-slot inventory. Brands do not.

Tickets
  • 750 Essence — Arcane Signet Ticket (free Arcane Signet, 1 slot)
  • 1500 Essence — Sol Ring Ticket (free Sol Ring, 1 slot)
  • 1500 Essence — Leyline Ticket (free Leyline, 1 slot)
  • 2000 Essence — Color Combo Ticket (choose color identity before commander generation, 1 slot)
  • 2500 Essence — Trinket Ticket (choose 1 of 3 Trinkets, 1 slot)
  • 4000 Essence — Conspiracy Ticket (choose 1 of 3 Conspiracies, 1 slot)
  • 5000 Essence — Vanguard Ticket (choose 1 of 3 Vanguards, 2 slots)
  • 10000 Essence — Emblem Ticket (choose 1 of 3 Emblems, 3 slots)
Brands
  • 500 Essence — Brand of the Cartographer: when drafting, replace 2 basic lands with dual lands or triomes.
  • 1000 Essence — Brand of the Conclave / Additional Commander Choice Brand: gain 1 extra commander choice during deckbuilding and at The Guild.
  • 1250 Essence — Brand of Recurrence / Additional Commander Reroll Brand: gain 1 extra free commander reroll during deckbuilding and at The Guild.
  • 1750 Essence — Brand of the Open Hand / +1 Extra Card in Pack Brand: every pack has 1 extra card to choose from.
  • 2000 Essence — Brand of the Blinded Eye / Commander Color Lockout Brand: exclude 1 color when rolling commanders.
  • 500 Essence — Brand of the Infinite Void: your deck size minimum is reduced by 1 per Rank.
  • Brands stack, have ranks, and each additional purchase costs base price multiplied by the current rank.
End-of-Game Options
  • 250 Essence — Sell Buffs
  • 500 Essence base — Capture Non-Commander, increasing by 250 Essence for each additional capture
